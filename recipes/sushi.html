Sushi

«

Rice and seaweed, soy and salmon

Sushi is a traditional Japanese of prepared vinegared rice, usually with some sugar and salt, accompanied by a variety of ingredients, such as seafood, often raw, and vegetables.

Styles of Sushi and its presentation vary widely, but the one key indredient is "Sushi Rice", also referred to as shari, or sumeshi.

Ingredients

For Sushi Rice

  • 2 CupsShortgrain rice
  • 3 CupsWater
  • Half a CupRice wine vinegar
  • 1 TablespoonVegetable Oil
  • Quarter CupWhite sugar
  • 1 teaspoon Salt

For Sushi Rolls

  • Nori (Dried seaweed sheets)
  • Crabsticks
  • Salmon
  • Cucumber
  • Carrots
  • Daikon radish
  • Kewpie Sushi Mayonnaise
  • Sesame seeds, regular and black

Steps for Rice

  1. Rinse the rice in a strainer or colander until the water runs clear.
  2. Combine with water in a medium saucepan. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover and cook for 20 minutes.
  3. Rice should be tender and water should be absorbed.
  4. Cool until cool enough to handle.
  5. In a small saucepan, combine the rice vinegar, oil, sugar and salt.
  6. Cook over medium heat until the sugar dissolves.
  7. Cool, then stir into the cooked rice. When you pour this in to the rice it will seem very wet. Keep stirring and the rice will dry as it cools.

Steps for Rolls

  1. Prepare your Sushi Mat, as a tip, cover the mat with cling wrap for a less messy experience!
  2. Place a Nori sheet onto your mat.
  3. Place a fistful of Sushi rice on the sheet and flatten gently into a square shape covering all visible areas of the sheet.
  4. Place your desired fillings onto the rice; carrot, cucumber, radish and crabsticks is a great combination.
  5. Start at end of the mat closest to you, and carefully but tightly roll the mat away from you in order to form your sushi rolls
  6. Now that you have a roll, dip the edge of a sharp nice into water and slice your rolls into even sections - a standard Nori sheet will yield 8 pieces of sushi, but slice as many as you can manage.
  7. Repeat steps 1 - 6 how many times you'd like.
  8. Arrange your sushi neatly on a plate, sprinkle sesame seeds and top with a tiny squeeze of Kewpie.
  9. Serve and Enjoy!
